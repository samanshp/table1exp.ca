Three things, done
very well.

N°1—N°3
N°1

The dough.

72-hour cold-fermented sourdough, mixed in our Vancouver kitchen the morning of your event. Hand-stretched on a marble counter we bring with us. No frozen, no premade, no shortcuts.

N°2

The fire.

A portable Italian oven that runs at 800°F, set up wherever your event is. Each pizza takes 90 seconds. Your guests watch them char, blister, and come out blistering hot — the closest thing to a wood-fire oven without the smoke.

N°3

The service.

One chef firing, one runner serving. We arrive 90 minutes early, bring everything we need, and clean up so it’s like we were never there. Your only job is to enjoy the night.

The night, in motion.

A typical evening
−90 min.

We arrive.

Setup is silent and out of the way. Oven heats while we prep the topping station. By the time your first guest walks in, the smell of woodfire is already in the air.

0:00.

First pizza out.

We start firing the moment your event opens. Pies come out one every 90 seconds, sliced and placed on the chef’s table for guests to grab as they walk by.

+45 min.

The crowd settles in.

The line of curious guests around the oven becomes the social hub of your event. People meet at the table. Conversation moves around the fire. This is the part nobody plans for and everyone remembers.

+2 hr.

Last pies, then we vanish.

We wind down quietly, pack the oven while guests linger, and leave the space exactly the way we found it. Most clients never see us pack up — that’s the point.

How much space do you need?
A 10×10 ft footprint outdoors, level ground, within 30 ft of your event space. Indoor venues can work too — ask us about the electric oven option.
Can you handle dietary restrictions?
Yes. Every package includes vegetarian and vegan options by default. Gluten-free dough available with 7 days notice. We label everything clearly at the station.
What about rain?
We bring our own tent. Vancouver weather doesn’t stop us — but if your event is fully exposed and forecast looks rough, we’ll work with you on a backup plan.
How far in advance should we book?
Summer Saturdays book 3–4 months out. Most other dates we can accommodate with 4–6 weeks notice. Last-minute? Send us a note — we hold a few flex dates each month.
Do you serve drinks?
Not directly — we focus on what we do best. We have a list of bar partners we love and can introduce you, or we’ll work seamlessly with the team you’ve already hired.

The team
behind the fire.

Table One is a two-person operation, by design.

We’re husband and wife — one of us behind the oven, the other running the floor. We started Table One because we wanted to build the kind of catering experience we’d actually want at our own wedding: warm, unhurried, and built around great food made in front of you.

Every event we book, we run ourselves. No subcontractors, no rotating staff. The people you meet at your discovery call are the same people firing pizzas at your event.
